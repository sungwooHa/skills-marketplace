# Korean AI-Tell Taxonomy v2.0.0 (AI 한글 티 분류 체계)

A catalogue of the "AI tells" that recur in Korean prose generated by LLMs (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and others), organized into 10 top-level categories with sub-patterns. This file is the single source of truth (SSOT) shared by the detector, the rewriter, and the reviewer. Each pattern carries (1) a definition, (2) a signature example, (3) a severity rating (S1 critical / S2 high / S3 low), and (4) a rewriting prescription.

> **v2.0.0 (2026-05-07):** Integrates the Korean translation-studies canon of the eight major translationese types (이영옥 2001 · 김도훈 2009 · 김정우 2007 · 김혜영 2019, among others) together with the eight types set out in §III.3 of the source report. **Four new patterns** — `A-16` 영어 대명사 직역 [S1] · `A-18` 관계절 좌향 수식 [S2] · `A-19` 이중 조사 결합 [S2] · `E-7` 청자 경어법 일관성 손실 [S2 · estimated]. **Four reinforced patterns** — `A-15` gains prescriptions for the cognition/utterance-verb split construction · `A-7` generalized to light verb constructions (have/make/take/give) · `F-4` absorbs the English nominalizing suffixes (-tion·-ment·-ness·-ity) · `E-2` gains a prescription for the automatic 진행형 '~고 있다' mapping. **One pattern on hold** — `A-17` 무정물·추상명사 '-들' 부착 [scholarly support is strong, but external rounds produced zero positives → re-evaluate in v2.1 after a round on raw NMT output]. **The three post-editese axes run as a metric-only track** — under caveat C3 (no quantitative validation for Korean) they receive no pattern ID and are operated through the 14 new functions in `metrics_v2.py` (including `deul_overuse_rate`, which keeps measuring the A-17 hold). The full scholarly apparatus lives in a companion file that ships beside this one as `scholarship.md`, keeping this rulebook slim. Valid as of 2026-05.
>
> **v1.6 (2026-05-06):** Five new patterns grounded in external quantitative research (KatFish, Park et al. + LREAD) — `C-11` 연결어미 뒤 쉼표 [S1, 4.84배 분리도] · `C-12` 쉼표 포함률 [S2] · `E-5` 쉼표 분절 평균 길이 [S2] · `E-6` 쉼표 전후 POS 다양성 [S2, essays and news only] · `G-3` 안전 균형 lexicon [S2]. Two candidates were held out of the taxonomy: BN/VX spacing regularity and persona–register mismatch.

## Severity criteria

- **S1 critical**: a single occurrence is nearly enough to convince a reader "this is AI." Always remove.
- **S2 high**: one or two occurrences can read naturally, but three or more in a document give it away. Remove on a density basis.
- **S3 low**: harmless in isolation. Strengthens the AI impression only when stacked with other patterns. Treat as rhythm tuning.

## A. 번역투 (Translation-ese) — S1~S2

Traces of English or Japanese syntax forced onto Korean word order and its particle system. The single most decisive signature of AI-written Korean.

### A-1. "~에 대하여/대해서" 남발 [S1]
- Definition: `X에 대해(서) Y`, a calque of English `about/regarding X`.
- Example: "AI 규제**에 대해** 논의할 필요가 있다" → "AI 규제를 논의해야 한다"
- Fix: connect directly with the object particle, or use the topic particle "는".

### A-2. "~를 통하여/통해" 남발 [S1]
- Definition: nearly every means or channel is rendered with "통해" (English `through/via`).
- Example: "데이터 분석**을 통해** 인사이트를 얻는다" → "데이터를 분석해 인사이트를 얻는다"
- Fix: spread the load across "~로", "~해서", "~함으로써", and similar forms.

### A-3. "~에 있어(서)" [S1]
- Definition: introduces a premise or situation (English `in terms of / when it comes to`).
- Example: "이 문제**에 있어서** 중요한 것은" → "이 문제에서 중요한 것은" / "이 문제를 볼 때"

### A-4. "~라는 점에서" [S2]
- Definition: presents grounds or rationale (English `in the sense that`).
- Example: "확장성이 뛰어나**다는 점에서** 의미가 있다" → "확장성이 뛰어나서 의미가 있다"
- Note: sometimes perfectly natural. Remove only when it repeats 3+ times in one document.

### A-5. "~와 관련하여" / "~와 관련된" [S2]
- Definition: points at a topic (English `regarding / related to`).
- Example: "보안**과 관련하여** 주의해야 한다" → "보안에 주의해야 한다"

### A-6. "~에 기반하여" / "~을 바탕으로" 남발 [S2]
- Definition: states grounds (English `based on`).
- Example: "데이터**에 기반하여** 판단한다" → "데이터로 판단한다" / "데이터를 보고 판단한다"

### A-7. "가지고 있다" [S1]
- Definition: describes possession or a property (English `have/possess`).
- Example: "강한 경쟁력을 **가지고 있다**" → "경쟁력이 강하다"
- Fix: shift to an adjectival predicate and drop the verb, or simplify to "있다".
- **Light verb construction generalization (v2.0 reinforcement)**: T6 of the source report covers light verb constructions in general — 'have/make/take/give + noun'. On top of the A-7 prescription, add explicitly (a) reduction back to a verb and (b) use of the double-subject construction ('X는 Y가 …'). Verbatim examples — "She has a sweet voice → 그녀는 목소리가 아름답다" / "She has a book under her arm → 그녀는 책을 옆구리에 끼고 있다" / "We had a meeting yesterday → 우리는 어제 회의를 했다(열었다)" / "The committee made a decision → 위원회가 결정했다" / "The data show a rapid increase → 데이터에 따르면 급격히 증가했다". Quantified by the metric `have_make_literal_count`. _source_anchor: 김정우 2007 · 이근희 2005 · see_scholarship: scholarship.md#6-명사화-표현-및-havemake-류-직역_

### A-8. 이중 피동 "~되어진다" / "~지게 된다" [S1]
- Example: "판단**되어진다**" → "판단된다" / "판단한다"
- Fix: go active where possible. If that fails, use a single passive.

### A-9. "~에 의해" 피동문 [S2]
- Definition: by-passive, a literal rendering of the English passive.
- Example: "AI**에 의해** 생성된 이미지" → "AI가 만든 이미지"
- Fix: restore the agent as the subject.

### A-10. "~할 수 있다" 남발 [S2]
- Definition: potential-mood predicates everywhere (English `can/be able to`).
- Example: "효율을 높**일 수 있다**. 비용을 줄**일 수 있다**. 시간을 단축**할 수 있다**."
- Fix: switch to assertive statements ("높인다", "줄인다"). Keep the potential form only where possibility is genuinely the point.

### A-11. "~을 위해" 목적절 남발 [S2]
- Definition: `X을 위해 Y한다`, a calque of English `in order to`.
- Example: "고객 만족**을 위해** 노력한다" → "고객이 만족하도록 일한다"

### A-12. "만들어지다" / "이루어지다" [S2]
- Definition: reflexive, agentless passives.
- Example: "합의가 **이루어졌다**" → "합의했다" / "합의에 이르렀다"

### A-13. 명사 나열 (조사 생략) [S2]
- Definition: English-style noun phrases strung together with no particles.
- Example: "AI 기술 발전 속도 가속화" → "AI 기술의 발전 속도가 빨라지고 있다"
- Fix: restore the appropriate particles ("의", "가", "를").

### A-14. 접속부사 "그리고" 절 연결 [S2]
- Definition: joining plain clauses with "그리고" the way English uses `and`.
- Example: "그는 보고했다. **그리고** 자리에 앉았다." → "그는 보고하고 자리에 앉았다."
- Fix: compress with connective endings such as "-고", "-며", "-면서".

### A-15. 추상 주어 + 만능 동사 [S2] · v1.1 new
- Definition: a literal rendering of English `The X shows / provides / brings Y`. The subject is an event or phenomenon and the predicate is "보여준다·제공한다·가져온다·시사한다".
- Example: "DeepSeek-V4**의 등장은** ~을 **보여줍니다**" / "이 전략**은** 지형**을 흔들고 있습니다**" / "X**는** Y**를 제공합니다**"
- Fix: return the subject to an actual agent (person, team, company), or drop subject and verb altogether and state the thing directly — along the lines of "DeepSeek는 ~ 원칙을 이렇게 증명했다".
- **v2.0 reinforcement — three prescriptions for causative, cognition, and utterance verbs**:
  - (a) Causative transitives (`X made Y …`) → reduce to an adverbial or causal clause: `X 때문에/덕분에 Y는 …` or `X로 인해 Y는 …`. Examples: "The news made him happy → 그 소식을 듣고 그는 기뻤다"; "1997년 금융위기는 한국 노동시장에 급격한 변화를 가져왔다 → 1997년 금융위기로 한국 노동시장은 급격히 바뀌었다"
  - (b) Cognition and utterance verbs (suggest/show/indicate/reveal) → split into `…에 따르면 …이다` or `…으로 …이 드러났다`. Example: "Recent research suggests that … → 최근 연구에 따르면 …이다 / 최근 연구를 통해 …이 드러났다"
  - (c) 'This book has 300 pages' and its kin → use the double-subject construction ('이 책은 300쪽이다', not '이 책은 300쪽을 가진다')
- _source_anchor: 이영옥 2001 · 김정우 2007 · see_scholarship: scholarship.md#1-무생물-주어--타동사-구문_

### A-16. 영어 대명사 직역 (그/그녀/그것/그들) [S1] · v2.0 new
- Definition: mapping English `he/she/it/they → 그/그녀/그것/그들` one-to-one. Korean secures cohesion through (i) zero-pronoun omission, (ii) reuse of the full noun phrase, and (iii) kinship or status terms of address. Korean '그/그녀' are closer to artificial coinages introduced through 19th–20th-century translated literature. Pronoun density in NMT/LLM output runs **2~3배** that of non-translated Korean (source report §3.3.3, verbatim).
- Examples:
  - "John was tired. He sat down. He sighed. He looked at his watch." → literal "존은 피곤했다. **그는** 앉았다. **그는** 한숨을 쉬었다. **그는** **그의** 시계를 보았다." → natural "존은 피곤했다. 자리에 앉아 한숨을 쉬고는 시계를 보았다."
  - "Mary called her mother because she missed her." → literal "메리는 **그녀가** **그녀를** 그리워해서 **그녀의** 어머니에게 전화했다." → natural "메리는 어머니가 그리워서 전화를 걸었다."
  - "his hand / her hair" → literal "**그의** 손 / **그녀의** 머리" → natural "손 / 머리" (almost always redundant)
- Fix:
  - (a) Treat 50~70% of pronoun occurrences as deletion candidates and restructure the sentence.
  - (b) Make the referent explicit with a noun phrase or term of address only at speaker or scene transitions.
  - (c) When 'he/she' refers to a person of unknown gender, use '그 사람' or drop the subject. Render 'they' not as '그들' but variously as '사람들·우리·일부·어떤 이들'.
- Detection threshold: score when personal pronouns appear ≥3 times within one paragraph (pe_checklist PE4). Add a peak score when the metric `pronoun_density` exceeds z>2.0 against the non-translated Korean baseline.
- _source_anchor: 김도훈 2009 통역과 번역 11(2): 3-19; Cho·Kim·Kim·Kim 2019 ACL GeBNLP arXiv:1905.11684 · see_scholarship: scholarship.md#3-대명사-직역-hesheitthey--그그녀그것그들_

### A-18. 관계대명사절 직역 — 긴 좌향 수식 (관형구 3중 이상 중첩) [S2] · v2.0 new
- Definition: English places relative clauses after the noun (right-branching); Korean places adnominal clauses before it (left-branching). Mapping a long English relative clause one-to-one explodes the reader's working-memory load before the head noun arrives. NMT and LLM systems try to preserve English SVO structure wherever they can, so left-branching modifiers pile up (박옥수 2018).
- Examples:
  - "He met a man who had once worked for the company that produced the chemical that caused the accident." → literal "그는 **사고를 일으킨 화학물질을 생산한 회사에서 한때 일했던 한 남자를** 만났다." → natural "그는 한 남자를 만났는데, 그 남자는 사고를 일으킨 화학물질을 만든 회사에서 한때 일했던 사람이었다." (relative clause moved into a postposed appositive)
  - "He was too intelligent and perceptive not to feel the disappointment of his admirers from the 1930s." → literal "그는 **1930년대부터 자기를 따랐던 사람들이 느낄 실망감을 눈치채지 못하기에는** 너무 똑똑하고 예민했다." → natural "그는 워낙 똑똑하고 예민해서 1930년대부터 자기를 따랐던 사람들이 느낄 실망감을 눈치챘다."
- Fix:
  - (a) When the relative clause runs to three 어절 or more, split the sentence or convert it into a postposed appositive.
  - (b) Do not render 'who, which, that' literally as '~인 X' or '~한 X'; unpack them as '~는데, ~으며, 그 X는'.
  - (c) When reviewing NMT output, any sentence with three or more stacked adnominal phrases — the '~한 …의 …을 …한 …이/가' shape — is an unconditional candidate for restructuring.
- Detection threshold: score when an adnominal phrase before a noun reaches ≥3 어절 (pe_checklist PE6). Count sentences where the metric `relative_clause_nesting` (adnominal nesting depth within one noun phrase) is ≥3, and score when the document exceeds one such sentence. **A-18 measures left-branching adnominal units, while E-5 (쉼표 분절 평균 길이) measures comma-delimited units — separate measurement dimensions. Weight upward when both are violated at once.**

### A-19. 이중 조사 결합 (-에서의·-에로의·-으로의·-에의·-으로부터의·-로부터의) [S2] · v2.0 new
- Definition: under the influence of Japanese 'の(の/への/での)' and English prepositional phrases ('of/in/to/from'), modern Korean accumulated expressions that stack two or three case particles. Korean natively prefers to unpack these into clauses or phrases.
- **Explicitly excluded under caveat C5 — plain '~의' is not a detection target**: there is no scholarly consensus on whether '~의' itself is Japanese-influenced translationese. The National Institute of Korean Language and 김슬옹, head of the Sejong Korean Language and Culture Institute, hold that '~의' has existed in Korean since the 15th century (source report caveat #5, verbatim). This pattern treats only the doubled combinations '~에서의·~에로의·~으로의·~에의·~으로부터의·~로부터의' as S2 or above.
- Examples:
  - "the meeting in the upper story of the bar" → literal "주점의 2층**에서의** 살림" → natural "주점의 2층에서 시작한 살림"
  - "liberation from tension" → literal "긴장**으로부터의** 해방" → natural "긴장에서 벗어남, 긴장이 풀림"
  - "the response to the questionnaire" → literal "설문지**에의** 응답" → natural "설문지에 대한 응답, 설문 답변"
  - "destroyed by the bombing" → literal "폭격에 의해 끊어진" / "이번 기회를 통하여" → natural "폭격으로 끊어진 / 이번 기회에"
- Fix:
  - (a) Treat every doubled particle ('-에서의/-에로의/-으로의/-에의/-으로부터의/-로부터의') as a search-and-sweep target.
  - (b) Do not map the prepositions 'from/to/through/by/of' one-to-one; reinterpret the meaning at sentence level.
  - (c) A run of '의 의 의' is almost always wrong — unpack it into clauses or phrases.
- Detection threshold: the metric `double_particle_count` matches the regex `에서의|에로의|으로의|에의|으로부터의|로부터의`. Score S2 when a document exceeds 3 occurrences. Estimated baseline for non-translated Korean: 0~2 occurrences.
- _source_anchor: 김정우 2007 번역학연구 8(1): 61-82; 김순영 2012 새국어생활 22(1) · see_scholarship: scholarship.md#7-일본어영어식-조사-결합-에서의에로의으로의에의_

## B. 영어 인용·용어 과다 (Excessive English Citation and Terminology) — S2

### B-1. 괄호 병기 관습 [S2]
- Definition: every technical term gets its English gloss in parentheses on first appearance.
- Example: "인공지능**(AI)**은 거대언어모델**(LLM)**과 다르다."
- Fix: for an expert readership, gloss once and then use Korean only. For a general readership, minimize English glossing outright.

### B-2. 영어 용어 비번역 [S2]
- Definition: leaving `pipeline`, `framework`, `leverage`, `seamless`, `robust` and the like in English when Korean would do.
- Example: "이 **framework**를 **leverage**하여" → "이 체계를 활용해"
- Exception: proper nouns and industry-standard terms (Transformer, API, SDK) stay as they are.

### B-3. 과도한 영어 인용구 [S2]
- Definition: English sentences dropped in verbatim as quotations, with a translation alongside.
- Fix: unless the original phrasing genuinely matters, paraphrase in Korean and cite only the source.

### B-4. "~라고 알려진", "~로 일컬어지는" [S3]
- Definition: a literal rendering of English `known as / so-called`.
- Example: "**'AGI'라고 알려진** 범용 인공지능" → "범용 인공지능(AGI)"

## C. 구조적 AI 패턴 (Structural AI Patterns — Formatting and Layout) — S1~S2

### C-1. 기계적 병렬 열거 [S1]
- Definition: "첫째, ~. 둘째, ~. 셋째, ~." dominates the whole paragraph.
- Fix: dissolve one or two of the three into running prose, or vary the wording with "우선 / 다음으로 / 마지막으로". If the enumeration is genuinely needed, keep it but deliberately break up the length and structure of each item.

### C-2. 과도한 불릿 리스트 [S2]
- Definition: three or more consecutive bullet blocks in an essay, column, or report.
- Fix: "dissolve" the bullets into prose. Keep only the points where a list genuinely carries meaning.

### C-3. 반복적 섹션 헤딩 [S2]
- Definition: schematic segmentation like `## 도입 ## 본론 ## 결론`.
- Fix: in prose writing, remove the headings entirely. In report writing, make the heading text concrete ("AI 규제의 세 가지 균열점").

### C-4. 문단 첫 문장 요약 공식 [S2]
- Definition: every paragraph opens with a topic sentence summarizing itself — the English composition-textbook formula.
- Fix: shuffle the order so some paragraphs open with a case, a scene, or a quotation.

### C-5. 이모지 남발 [S1]
- Definition: emoji such as `✅ 🚀 💡 ⚠️ 📊` planted at list heads, in headings, and on emphasis.
- Fix: in essay or report contexts, remove all of them. Outside social media and product copy, the AI tell here is extreme.

### C-6. 헤딩 아래 한 줄 요약 박스 [S2]
- Definition: a guide sentence such as "이 섹션에서는 ~를 다룬다" right after every section heading.
- Fix: delete. Korean prose reads properly when the body starts immediately.

### C-7. 문단 간 기계적 "먼저·반면·결국" 3단 공식 [S2] · v1.1 new
- Definition: paragraph openings locked into "먼저 ~ / 반면 ~ / 결국 ~" or "첫째 ~ / 둘째 ~ / 마지막으로 ~" in sequence. Korean writers use this occasionally, but three or more in a row is distinctly AI.
- Example: the v1 draft of this document (paragraph 2 "먼저", paragraph 3 "반면", paragraph 6 "결국").
- Fix: delete two of the three. Let the ordering come through in the flow of the paragraphs themselves. Mix in paragraphs that open with no connective at all.

### C-8. 대칭 대구 공식 "A인가, B인가" 반복 [S2] · v1.1 new
- Definition: a binary opposition repeated as a parallel construction three or more times in the same document. A literal import of English rhetoric.
- Example: "독점**인가**, 확산**인가**" / "전략가에게는 ~, 입안자에게는 ~" / "누가 더 ~, 누가 더 ~"
- Fix: rearrange two of the three asymmetrically. Make one side a question and the other a statement, or spell one side out at length and keep the other short.

### C-9. 숫자 괄호 인덱싱 "1) 2) 3)" [S2] · v1.3 new
- Definition: items listed as `1) ... 2) ... 3) ...` within one paragraph or across adjacent sentences. A notational signature distinct from C-1 (첫째·둘째·셋째) and C-2 (bullets). Korean writers do use it in reports, but LLM output reaches for numeric-parenthesis indexing almost automatically whenever there are three items, at an overwhelmingly higher rate.
- Example: "**1)** 표준화된 인프라가 일반화되면서 학습 데이터 확보가 용이해졌다. **2)** 도메인 특화 LLM이 성숙하면서 비정형 텍스트의 활용 범위가 넓어졌다. **3)** 클라우드 GPU 단가가 크게 하락하면서 자체 학습이 비용 측면에서 정당화 가능한 수준에 들어왔다."
- Fix: dissolve one of the three into prose and vary the other two with "우선~", "다음으로~" instead of the 1)·2) notation. Keep numeric parentheses only where an identically structured list genuinely matters, and at most once per document.

### C-10. 콜론 부제 헤딩 공식 "X: Y" 또는 "X: A에서 B로" [S2] · v1.3.1 new
- Definition: headings almost automatically structured with a colon as "main label: subtitle" or "main label: topic noun phrase". Adjacent to C-3 (repetitive headings) but distinct — C-3 is schematic segmentation (`## 도입 ## 본론 ## 결론`), while C-10 plants a meta label plus colon plus subtitle in the heading itself. A Gemini-dominant signature (verified in round 3).
- Example: "### **서론**: 제조업의 미래, AI에 달려있다" / "### **본론 1**: 빛과 그림자, 대기업과 중소기업의 디지털 격차" / "## 2026년 핀테크, '일상'을 넘어 '생태계'로 진화하다"
- Fix: strip the colon and subtitle from the heading and compress it into a single noun or verb phrase. If a subtitle is genuinely needed, either (a) fold it into the first sentence of the body or (b) use an em dash or a line break instead of a colon. At most one colon-subtitle heading per document.

### C-11. 연결어미 뒤 쉼표 [S1] · v1.6 new
- Definition: a comma immediately after a connective ending (-고/-며/-지만/-면서/-아서·어서/-자/-는데). For Korean writers the connective ending itself creates the breath unit, so an extra comma is almost never needed; AI transplants the English sense of a comma after a conjunction and inserts one automatically. This is the strongest single discriminator in the entire taxonomy (KatFish essays: human 4.10% vs AI 19.83%, **4.84배**).
- Example: "AI는 빠르게 발전하**지만,** 기업의 대응은 더디다" / "데이터를 정제하**고,** 모델을 학습시킨**다음,** 결과를 검증한다" / "비용이 낮아지**면서,** 진입장벽이 사라졌다"
- Fix: sweep out every comma following a connective ending. If the breath then runs too long, split the clause the Korean way (break with a period) or move the comma to another position (a main-clause boundary). Score S1 at 6+ occurrences per document, S2 at 3~5.

### C-12. 쉼표 포함률 (문서 단위) [S2] · v1.6 new
- Definition: more than 50% of all sentences contain at least one comma. A different measurement dimension from C-11 (which is about positional idiosyncrasy after connective endings) — C-12 covers document-wide distribution. AI tends to put a comma in nearly every sentence (KatFish essays: human 26.31% vs AI 61.03%, 2.32배).
- Example: four or five of the five sentences in a paragraph all carrying commas (the human average is one or two out of five).
- Fix: when the share of comma-bearing sentences exceeds 50%, convert some of them by (a) splitting at the comma into short sentences, (b) absorbing the comma into a connective ending, or (c) simply deleting it. Score at z>1.0 against a 26~33% baseline.

## D. AI 특유의 관용구 (Signature Phrases) — S1

Stock phrases Korean writers almost never use but LLMs produce over and over. Replace on sight.

### D-1. 종결·요약류
- "결론적으로", "요약하면", "종합하면", "정리하자면"
- "~라고 할 수 있다" / "~라고 볼 수 있다"
- "~라 하겠다", "~라 할 것이다"
- "~에 다름 아니다"
- **KatFish-validated closing lexicon, four items (v1.6 reinforcement)**: "결론적으로 / 따라서 / 이를 통해 / 그러므로" — indicator 5 of the six lexicon-grounded measures in the Park et al. report. In the LREAD Phase 2 rubric this was the key item that lifted human judgment accuracy from 60% to 90%. When these four together exceed 3 occurrences in a document, strengthen the D-1 score (severity stays S1). "이를 통해" also scores under A-2 (~를 통해 남발), and "따라서·그러므로" also score under H-1 (sentence-initial connectives).

### D-2. 의의·중요성 과장
- "매우 중요하다", "반드시 기억해야 한다"
- "시사하는 바가 크다", "주목할 만하다"
- "간과할 수 없다", "무시할 수 없다"
- "~의 지평을 연다", "~에 방점을 찍는다"
- "그 의미가 적지 않다", "의미심장하다"

### D-3. 열거 도입
- "크게 세 가지로 나눌 수 있다"
- "다음과 같은 특징을 가진다"
- "다음과 같이 요약할 수 있다"

### D-4. AI 티 특화
- "혁신적인", "획기적인", "전례 없는" (hype vocabulary)
- **Gemini-dominant hype vocabulary set (v1.3.1 reinforcement)**: "압도적·막강한·폭발적·파격적·대대적·강력한·치열한·뜨거운"
  - "**압도적** 1위 사업자는 ~ **막강한** 월간 활성 이용자(MAU)"
  - "**파격적인** 예적금 금리"
  - "**폭발적인** 호응을 얻고 있다"
  - "**대대적인** 개편이 필요합니다"
- "~의 가능성을 열어준다"
- "~의 새로운 장을 열다"
- "~시대가 도래했다"

### D-5. 의인화된 추상 주어 [S2] · v1.1 new
- Definition: taking an event, technology, or concept as the subject and describing it as if it acted like a person. A default move when AI tries to make writing look "weighty".
- Example: "**두 지능의 충돌**이 질문을 **던집니다**" / "**AI 대전**이 **끝나지 않습니다**" / "**지능의 가성비**가 **증명합니다**"
- Fix: swap in a real agent as the subject ("두 회사의 경쟁은", "엔지니어들은"), or weaken the personifying verb ("던집니다"→"남습니다"·"생깁니다"). One instance in a symbolic title or summary is acceptable.

### D-6. 완결 공식형 결말 "~할 때입니다 / 시점입니다" [S2] · v1.1 new
- Definition: the closing sentence of a column or report locked into "~해야 할 때입니다", "~로 나아갈 시점입니다", "~할 순간입니다".
- Example: "에이전트 정부의 시대로 **나아가야 할 때입니다**"
- Fix: assert the same meaning with a concrete verb. Something like "에이전트 정부 단계로 **넘어갈 때입니다**" is acceptable (less inflated). Once per document at most.

### D-7. 변환 공식 "X에서 Y로 / X을 넘어 Y로" [S2] · v1.3.1 new
- Definition: reached for almost automatically when expressing a paradigm shift, evolution, or upgrade. A closing/slogan formula distinct from D-1, D-2, and D-6, and a different signature from C-8 (A인가 B인가, interrogative) — this one emphasizes **the direction of the shift**. A Gemini-dominant signature (verified in round 3, 7 occurrences across 2 domains).
- Example: "**'규모의 경쟁'에서 '전략의 경쟁'으로**", "**'지식 전달자'에서 '학습 조력자'로**", "**'무엇을'에서 '어떻게'로**", "**'데이터 조회'를 넘어 '맞춤형 금융 비서'로**"
- Fix: turn the shift formula into a direct assertion (for example "'지식 전달자'에서 '학습 조력자'로" → "교사는 더 이상 지식 전달자가 아니다. 학생 곁에서 학습을 돕는다"). At most one shift formula per document, and only in the closing passage when a paradigm shift really is the core message.

**Fix (section-wide):** mostly **delete**. Where the meaning is needed, substitute a concrete noun or verb ("중요하다"→"핵심이다", or concrete evidence).

## E. 리듬·문장 길이 균일성 (Rhythm and Sentence-Length Uniformity) — S2

### E-1. 문장 길이 표준편차 낮음
- Every sentence clusters around 30~50 characters.
- Fix: deliberately insert one or two short sentences (10~15 characters) per paragraph to vary the rhythm. Mix in one long sentence (80+ characters) as well.

### E-2. 동일 종결어미 반복
- "~이다. ~이다. ~이다."
- "~한다. ~한다. ~한다."
- Fix: mix "~다"·"~았다"·"~인 것" and nominal endings. Human writers vary these unconsciously.
- **v2.0 reinforcement — prescription for the automatic 진행형 '~고 있다' mapping**:
  - Automatically mapping the English progressive (be -ing) onto Korean '~고 있다' produces redundancy (source report §3.8.4, verbatim — both '지금 책을 읽는다' and '책을 읽고 있다' are possible, and the simple tense already carries progressive meaning).
  - Whenever '~고 있다' appears, check whether it can be reduced to the simple tense (pe_checklist PE10). Example: "I have been thinking about it. → 그동안 그 일을 곰곰이 생각해 봤다" (rejecting `~해 오고 있다`).
  - When four or more consecutive sentences in a paragraph end in '~다', diversify ('~었다·~ㄴ다·~는다·~기 마련이다·~ㄹ 것이다·~을 수 있다' and so on) — pe_checklist PE9.
  - Score when the metric `progressive_aspect_rate` (frequency of ~고 있다 / total sentences) exceeds 0.5.

### E-3. 모든 문단 3~4문장 공식
- Paragraph length is uniform too.
- Fix: deliberately mix in a one-sentence paragraph and a six-sentence paragraph.

### E-4. 단문 일변도 (복문·중문 부재) [S2] · v1.5.1 new
- Definition: nearly every sentence is simple (one subject-predicate pair), with almost no complex or compound sentences built from connective endings, adnominal clauses, or quotative clauses. Frequent in AI output that was told to "write concisely" or that aims for a short breath. Human writers unconsciously mix simple and complex sentences to create rhythm, so a run of nothing but simple sentences is itself the signature. A twin of E-1 — E-1 flags the standard deviation of length, E-4 flags monotony of structure.
- Example: "AI는 빠르게 발전한다. 기업은 따라가야 한다. 시간이 없다. 데이터가 핵심이다. 인재도 부족하다."
- Contrast (human tone): "AI가 빠르게 발전하는 가운데 기업은 따라가야 한다. 시간은 없고 데이터는 핵심이며, 인재마저 부족하다."
- Fix: bind two or three adjacent simple sentences into complex ones using connective endings ("-며·-고·-는데·-면서·-자"), adnominal clauses ("~하는 N", "~인 N"), quotative clauses, or conditional clauses. Tune the share of simple sentences to around 60% and complex/compound sentences to 30% or more. Use simple sentences deliberately, for emphasis, pivots, and knockout lines.

### E-5. 쉼표 분절 평균 길이 (긴 절 구조) [S2] · v1.6 new
- Definition: the average number of 어절 in comma-delimited clauses grows long. AI produces the English-style long sentence, chaining long subordinate clauses with commas inside a single sentence (KatFish essays: human 4.35어절 vs AI 8.56어절, 1.97배). A twin of E-4 but pointing the opposite way — too short is 단문 일변도, too long is the English long sentence; both extremes are AI signatures.
- Example: "AI 기술이 빠르게 발전하면서 산업 전반의 생산성이 높아지고 있는 가운데, 기업의 디지털 전환 속도가 가속화되고, 인재 확보 경쟁이 치열해지면서, 데이터 인프라 투자도 확대되고 있다." (four clauses in one sentence, each 8~12어절)
- Fix: score when average clause length exceeds 7어절. Split clauses of 8어절 or more with a period, or compress the English-style subordinate clause into a Korean adnominal clause. Weight upward when E-4 is violated at the same time.

### E-6. 쉼표 전후 POS 다양성 높음 (구문 복잡도) [S2] · v1.6 new · genre-gated
- Definition: the number of distinct parts of speech appearing before and after commas explodes. AI inserts commas indiscriminately at all kinds of POS boundaries (after subjects, adverbial clauses, parentheticals, conjunctions), driving POS diversity very high (KatFish essays: human 24.38 vs AI 59.39, 2.44배). **Genre gate — applies only to essays, news, blogs, QA, and reports.** Discrimination is weak in verse and literary genres such as poetry and fiction (poetry: human 23.13 vs AI 23.86, 1.03배).
- Example: within one document, commas inserted indiscriminately after nouns, adverbs, verbs, determiners, quotations, and conjunctions alike.
- Fix: restrict comma placement to (a) main-clause boundaries and (b) unmistakable appositives or parentheticals. No indiscriminate insertion. Score at z>1.0 against a baseline of roughly 24~30 (essays/news).

### E-7. 청자 경어법 일관성 손실 (해라/하게/하오/해요/합쇼체) [S2 · estimated] · v2.0 new
- Definition: Korean, as an agglutinative language, marks with its sentence-final endings (a) sentence type, (b) speech act, (c) modality, (d) politeness toward the listener, and even (e) the speaker–listener relationship (source report §3.8.1, verbatim). English has no such endings and expresses these through word order, verb inflection, and modal auxiliaries, so English-to-Korean translation and LLM output frequently lose listener-honorific consistency. **해라체·하게체·하오체·해요체·합쇼체 get mixed together within one document or conversation.** A different axis from E-2 (동일 종결어미 반복) — E-2 is monotonous repetition of a single ending, E-7 is loss of consistency in formality level.
- **estimated flag (caveat C1)**: the quantitative figures in 김혜영 2019 (percentage frequency of the declarative '-다') are inferred from the English abstract and keywords of KCI ART002506702. Thresholds stay marked 'estimated' until the PDF is obtained directly.
- Example: within one conversation, "도와주시겠습니까?(합쇼)" is suddenly followed by "도와줘?(해라)"; one paragraph of a report uses "~합니다"(합쇼) and the next jumps to "~한다"(해라); over-formal literalism such as "Will you help me? → 당신은 나를 도와주겠습니까?".
- Fix:
  - (a) Fix the listener level (해라/하게/해요/합쇼) at the start of the document and hold it consistently.
  - (b) Render "Will you help me?" with exactly one level appropriate to the relationship and politeness — "좀 도와주시겠어요? / 도와줄래?" and so on.
  - (c) Refuse to flatten speech acts and modality (might/may) into a single "~수 있다" — diversify with "~을지 모른다·~을 가능성도 있다·~을 수도 있겠다·~을 법하다".
- Detection threshold: **genre gate — applies only to dialogue and spoken text (fiction dialogue, interview transcripts, quoted dialogue inside essays).** Single-register formal genres such as reports and policy documents are exempt (already consistent in 합쇼체). Score S2 when two or more formality levels are found mixed within one document or conversation.

## F. 과도한 수식·중복 (Excessive Modification and Redundancy) — S2

### F-1. 정도부사 중독
- "매우", "정말", "진짜로", "대단히", "극히"
- Fix: delete most of them. Where emphasis is needed, substitute a concrete figure or example.

### F-2. 동의어 이중 수식
- "중요하고 핵심적인 역할"
- "새롭고 혁신적인 접근"
- "지속적이고 꾸준한 노력"
- Fix: keep only one of the two modifiers.

### F-3. 기능+역할 복합구
- "~로서의 역할과 기능"
- "~의 의미와 가치"
- Fix: keep one.

### F-4. 과잉 접두·접미
- "~적 측면", "~적 관점"
- "~성(性)", "~화(化)" 남발
- Example: "**근본적 관점**에서 **구조적 변화**가 **필연적**이다" → "구조가 근본부터 바뀐다"
- **Three Sino-Korean nominalizing suffixes named explicitly (v1.6 reinforcement)**: "-성(性) · -적(的) · -화(化)" — formally named as hanja_nominalizers in the KatFish report. **Strengthen to S2 when the density of words formed with these three exceeds 12 occurrences per document.** F-5 (the "~적 N" abstract-compound chain) stays separate as a special case of the "-적" suffix. The prescription is the same as here — decompose into (a) a verb or adjective root, or (b) a concrete noun.
- **Four English nominalizing suffixes absorbed (v2.0 reinforcement)**: literal Korean noun renderings of English noun phrases stacked with `-tion · -ment · -ness · -ity` fall under the same prescription. Example: "the implementation of the policy → 정책 시행" or "정책을 시행하기" (source report §3.6, verbatim prescription). When one of the four English nominalizations has been mapped one-to-one onto a Sino-Korean noun, reduce it to a verb or adjective root. The combined threshold for the three Sino-Korean suffixes (-성·-적·-화) plus the four English ones (-tion·-ment·-ness·-ity) remains the v1.6 threshold above: strengthen to S2 above 12 occurrences per document.

### F-5. "~적 N" 복합 추상어 체인 [S2] · v1.1 new
- Definition: the "~적 N" shape before a noun repeats three or more times in one document. Unlike F-4, this is not abstract adnominal use ("적 측면/관점") but a chain of **"~적 N" before concrete nouns**. Frequent when AI imitates the intellectual authority of a source text.
- Example: "에이전트**적** 자율성", "기술**적** 안정성", "경제**적** 자립", "기술**적** 토대", "시스템**적** 접근", "구조**적** 변화"
- Fix: decompose each "~적 N" into one of (a) "~로서의 N" ("에이전트로서의 자율성"), (b) a verb phrase ("기술이 얼마나 안정적인가"), or (c) a concrete noun ("토대"). Cut the document-wide density of "~적 N" by at least half.

## G. 과도한 Hedging (완곡) — S2

### G-1. 추측·관측형 종결
- "~할 수 있을 것으로 보인다"
- "~인 것으로 판단된다"
- "~라고 여겨진다"
- "~인 듯하다" attached to the end of every sentence
- Fix: assert where assertion is possible. Hedge only at genuinely uncertain points.

### G-2. 이중·삼중 완곡
- "~할 가능성이 있을 수 있다"
- "~로 보여질 수 있다"
- Fix: keep only one hedge.

### G-3. 안전 균형 lexicon (Safe Balance Score) [S2] · v1.6 new
- Definition: high frequency of balance, both-sides, and softening vocabulary such as "양쪽 모두 / 두 가지 모두 / 장점도 있지만 / 신중하게 / 균형". Indicator 6 of the six lexicon-grounded measures in the KatFish report. A different measurement dimension from G-1 (speculative sentence-final endings) — G-1 works at the level of endings, G-3 at the level of lexicon. Directly tied to the "risk avoidance / both-sides presentation" item in the LREAD rubric.
- Example: "**양쪽 모두** 일리가 있다 / **두 가지 모두** 검토할 필요가 있다 / **장점도 있지만** 단점도 있다 / **신중하게** 접근해야 한다 / **균형** 잡힌 시각이 중요하다"
- Fix: convert balance vocabulary into (a) an assertion for one side, (b) a concrete case comparison, or (c) a conditional ("X일 때는 A, Y일 때는 B"). Replace "신중하게·균형" with concrete verbs and criteria. Score S2 when the five lexicon items together exceed 4 occurrences in a document. Applies to policy and report genres only (essays and poetry have different baselines — genre-specific reinforcement is future metric-engineering work).

## H. 접속사 남발 (Conjunction Overuse) — S2

### H-1. 문두 접속사 과다
- "또한", "따라서", "즉", "나아가", "아울러", "게다가", "더욱이" at the start of every sentence and every paragraph.
- Fix: remove 70% or more. The logical flow should emerge from the content of the sentences themselves. Keep them only where a contrast or causal link genuinely needs emphasis.

### H-2. "하지만"과 "그러나" 혼용 남발
- An adversative in every paragraph.
- Fix: delete more than half. When the contrast is self-evident, it works without a connective.

### H-3. "이는 ~" 지시 반복
- "이는 ~을 의미한다"
- **Meta-entry variants (v1.3 reinforcement)**: "이 점에서 ~ / 이 관점에서 보면 ~ / 이 말은 ~" — the same function as the core H-3 pattern (picking up the previous sentence to elaborate) in a differently formed construction.
  - "**이 관점에서 보면** AI 시대 유망 직무도 다시 보인다"
  - "**이 말은** 결국 기술 도입보다 인력 전환이 더 큰 병목이 될 수 있다는 뜻이다"
  - "**이 점에서** 앞으로 강해질 인재는 크게 다섯 부류다"
- Fix: attach to the previous sentence or replace with a concrete statement. Same prescription for the variants — delete the meta-entry and go straight into the substance.

### H-4. 재정의 접속사 "즉" 남발 [S2] · v1.1 new
- Definition: a literal rendering of English `i.e.` / `that is`. "즉" gets prefixed whenever a gloss is needed.
- Example: "AI 민주화, **즉** 경제성 측면에서"
- Fix: vary with "곧", "말하자면", "다시 말해", "바꿔 말하면". Or drop it entirely and join the two halves with a comma alone. Limit "즉" to 2 occurrences per document.

## I. 형식명사·의존명사 과다 (Bound-Noun Overuse) — S2

### I-1. "것이다" 종결 남발
- "~한 것이다", "~일 것이다" as the dominant ending in a paragraph.
- Fix: switch to assertive statements. End with "~다".

### I-2. "점", "바", "수", "데" 반복
- "주목할 **점**은", "나아갈 **바**는", "할 **수**가 있다", "하는 **데**에"
- **Compound variant (v1.3 reinforcement)**: the emphatic positional predicate "X은 ~라는 **점에 있다**"
  - "핵심은 진입장벽이 빠르게 낮아지고 있다**는 점에 있다**"
  - "의의는 표준화가 사업장별로 들쭉날쭉하다**는 점에 있다**"
  - "주목할 부분은 수익 모델이 정착되지 않았다**는 점에 있다**"
- Fix: replace with a concrete noun or verb, or delete. For the compound variant, go straight to an assertion in the form "X은 ~다".

### I-3. "~라는 것"
- "변화가 크**다는 것이다**."
- **Closing-assertion variant (v1.3 reinforcement)**: "~라는 뜻이다 / ~다는 뜻이다" — GPT reaches for this almost automatically when finishing a summarizing sentence with a bound noun.
  - "기술 도입보다 인력 전환이 더 큰 병목이 될 수 있**다는 뜻이다**"
  - "한국에서는 이 문제가 더 민감하**다는 뜻이다**"
  - "더 큰 시장은 응용 산업에서 나올 가능성이 높**다는 뜻이다**"
- Fix: "변화가 크다." (straight to the sentence-final ending). For the closing variant, end directly with "~다" (for example "병목은 인력 전환이다"). Limit bound-noun closings ("~다는 것이다 / ~다는 뜻이다 / ~다는 점이다") to 2 occurrences per document in total.

### I-4. "~할 필요가 있다" + 정책 보고서 권고형 결말
- A literal rendering of English `should/need to`.
- **Recommendation-closing variant (v1.3.1 reinforcement)**: "~해야 한다 / ~해야 합니다" appears automatically at the end of every policy document or report. **Strengthen to S2 above 5 occurrences per document** (a machine-generation signature).
  - "공유 플랫폼을 **구축해야 한다** / 바우처 지원 사업을 대폭 확대하여 ~ **낮춰야 한다** / 핵심 인재를 양성하는 것이 **중요하다**"
  - "균형을 **맞춰야 합니다** / **구축해야 합니다** / **마련해야 합니다** / **지원해야 합니다**"
- Fix: vary "~해야 한다 / ~해야 합니다" into one of (a) a concrete verb assertion ("~를 시급히 추진"), (b) a verb with an explicit agent ("정부는 ~를 도입한다"), or (c) a conditional ("~이 충족되면 ~가 가능하다"). Avoid more than 5 recommendation closings per document.

### I-5. "~이/가 필요하다"
- "혁신이 필요하다", "변화가 필요하다"
- Fix: make it concrete with a subject and a verb — who has to do what.

### I-6. "~능력" 추상명사 연쇄 [S2] · v1.1 new
- Definition: "N 능력" repeats three or more times in one document, describing capability with a noun phrase instead of a verb. Carries the flavor of English `ability to X / X capability` rendered literally.
- Example: "사고 **능력**", "워크플로우 수행 **능력**", "장기 문맥 유지 **능력**", "추론 **능력**"
- Fix: unpack into verb form. "사고 능력은 뛰어나다" → "잘 사고한다" / "사고의 수준이 높다". "워크플로우 수행 능력" → "워크플로우를 얼마나 잘 처리하는지". Limit "~능력" to 2 occurrences per document.

## J. 시각 장식 남용 (Visual Decoration Overuse) — S2~S3

### J-1. 과도한 **볼드**
- Key words bolded in every sentence.
- Fix: remove nearly all bolding from body text. It generates visual noise and nothing else.

### J-2. 따옴표 과다
- Quotation marks scattered over concept words and emphasis.
- **Frequency threshold named explicitly (v1.3.1 reinforcement)**: **strengthen to S2 above 5 quoted-emphasis items per document**. Gemini produces cases of 17~33 per document (for example: "'옥석 가리기'·'금융 슈퍼앱'·'데이터 피로감'·'규제 샌드박스'·'무대 위의 현자'·'곁에서 돕는 안내자'·'학습 경험 설계자'").
- Fix: restrict to genuine quotations and special usages. For concept emphasis, either (a) fold it into the flow of the prose or (b) use quotation marks once on first appearance and plain Korean thereafter.

### J-3. 대시(—) 남용
- English em-dash-style asides.
- Example: "AI는 도구 — 그 이상도 이하도 아닌 — 이다"
- Fix: break out into commas, parentheses, or a separate sentence. One or two per document at most.

### J-4. 괄호 부연 과다
- Repeated asides such as "(이는 ~을 의미한다)".
- Fix: move most parenthetical asides into the body text, or delete them.

## Detection output schema (detector → rewriter contract)

The detector produces the following JSON:

```json
{
  "meta": {
    "input_length": 1820,
    "detected_count": 37,
    "ai_tell_density": 0.203,
    "severity_weighted_score": 71.5
  },
  "findings": [
    {
      "id": "f001",
      "category": "A-2",
      "category_label": "번역투: ~를 통해 남발",
      "severity": "S1",
      "text_span": "데이터 분석을 통해",
      "start": 142,
      "end": 153,
      "reason": "'통해'가 본문에서 6회 반복되어 경로 서술이 기계적",
      "suggested_fix": "데이터를 분석해서"
    }
  ],
  "category_summary": {
    "A": 12, "B": 3, "C": 2, "D": 8, "E": 1,
    "F": 4, "G": 2, "H": 3, "I": 1, "J": 1
  }
}
```

- `severity_weighted_score`: weighted sum with S1=5, S2=2, S3=0.5, normalized to a 0~100 scale.
- `ai_tell_density`: total characters in detected spans / total characters.

## The three post-editese axes — metric-only track (introduced in v2.0)

> **Important**: the three post-editese axes (simplification, normalisation, interference) are operated without pattern IDs in this taxonomy. The reason is **caveat C3**, verbatim — "Toral(2019)은 en→de, de→en, es→de, en→fr, zh→en의 5개 언어쌍을 다뤘고, **한국어는 포함되지 않았다**. 한국어에 대한 동일 결론은 합리적 추론이지만 정량적 검증은 미수행 상태다." Pattern IDs in this taxonomy therefore carry only verifiable signals matchable at the token or syntactic level, and the composite three-axis signals are split off as metric-only.

The 14 new functions in `metrics_v2.py` operate the three axes (a `speculative: true` flag is recommended on every one of these metrics):

- **Simplification axis**: `lexical_diversity_ttr` · `lexical_density` · `ending_diversity` (Baker 1993; Toral 2019).
- **Normalisation axis**: `normalisation_score` (concentration of the declarative -다/된다/이다) · `da_streak_rate` (count of streaks of 4+ consecutive -다 sentences) (Baker 1993).
- **Interference axis**: eight detection signals T1~T8 plus the composite `interference_index` (Toury 1995, law of interference) — `inanimate_subject_rate` (T1↔A-15·D-5) · `by_passive_count`/`double_passive_count` (T2↔A-8·A-9·A-12) · `pronoun_density` (T3↔A-16) · `deul_overuse_rate` (T4↔A-17, **on hold; measurement retained for validation**) · `relative_clause_nesting` (T5↔A-18) · `have_make_literal_count` (T6↔A-7·F-4) · `double_particle_count` (T7↔A-19) · `progressive_aspect_rate` (T8↔E-2·E-7).

The link between taxonomy patterns and metrics runs both ways. When a pattern's violation count crosses its threshold, the detector and rewriter prescribe by pattern ID; at the same time, when a composite metric score is an outlier against baseline, the reviewer runs an additional check. Korean baselines are derived by the metric engineer from a non-translated Korean corpus (Sejong and similar).
